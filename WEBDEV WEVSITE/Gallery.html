AICE ICECREAM



1. MANGO SLUSH



Aice’s claim-to-fame lands the top of this ranking. Their mango slush tastes like a sweetened fresh ripe mango shake, frozen into a mango-shaped bar. It has a delicious real mango flavor; and some members of the team said it tasted as premium as Haagen Dazs’ Alphonso Mango flavor. The texture is also first-rate. It holds its shape, and is hard until you bite into it, then it becomes chewy and melts just enough to still be a mass in your mouth. The only downside is there’s extra plastic in the packaging, but if it’s necessary to keep that cool shape, then we might just forgive it.



2. SWEET CORN



Perhaps Aice’s most popular flavor here (or at least the most viral), their sweet corn variant has a soft, chewy, almost cake-y shell covering a sweet corn-flavored ice cream. All-in-all, it tastes—and smells—just like sweet corn. It might be a bit polarizing (we had a few back and forth’s within the team, too), but the ice cream is undeniably outstanding for the flavor it says it is.



3. COFFEE CRISPY



The coffee crispy has the same accurate coffee taste as the ice cream in torch coffee. “It tastes like the brown sugar Kopiko,” a member of the team described it. Plus, it also comes with a bonus chocolate shell, which is just as thick and delicious as the chocolate crispy’s. One of our team members chose this as her favorite (hint: it’s the author).



4. WATERMELON



One of Aice’s unique flavors, semangka is a watermelon-flavored popsicle. The flavor is pretty much like other watermelon sticks, though this one’s richer and tastes more premium. We also love how it looks like a slice of watermelon. It even has “seeds,” which are soft chocolate chips.



5. CHOCOLATE MOCHI



The chocolate mochi tastes the most like a real chocolate bar, making it the best our of Aice’s chocolate variants. That said, the chocolate here isn’t actually the ice cream but the ganache center (which was the best part) and the mochi outside; the ice cream is vanilla. Some members of the team also commented about the mochi’s bad UX, saying that the mochi was too sticky. Though, in a way, some of us found it more enjoyable.



6. CHOCOLATE CRISPY



Aice’s chocolate crispy, in our opinion, is the best you can get of the kind at the price point. It’s vanilla and milk ice cream, which you can actually tell once you bite into it. It starts with the milk taste, then ends with the vanilla flavor. The chocolate shell is thick (thanks to the double layer), yet still crunchy, and it doesn’t fall apart too easily.



7. MILK MELON



If you grew up eating those melon milk snacks from the grocery, then you’re gonna love Aice’s milk melon stick because it tastes exactly like that. Although, if you hated those, then this’ll suck for you. (Just don’t get it, geez.) The texture is in the middle of the brand’s frozen sticks and creamy ice cream variants. Though TBH, we wish it were creamier.



8. STRAWBERRY CRISPY



The strawberry crispy is strawberry ice cream covered in a white chocolate shell. The coating wasn’t bad; it was a bit thin, but we liked that it didn’t at all feel oily. The ice cream had a faint strawberry flavor (maybe they mixed in too much vanilla?), which was obviously fake. But it was “fake in a nostalgic way,” like all the strawberry-flavored food we had as kids.



9. CHOCOLATE



The chocolate stick kind of tastes like the chocolate sundae (in the cup), but way better. (Plus extra points because you don’t have to eat it with a spoon.) It’s much creamier, and tastes less artificial. It also has the same nice texture as the other stick variants. Members of the team liken the taste to “a creamier chocolate Twin Popsies,” “a Milo ice cream,” and “the creamy choco in our childhood.”



10. MILK



The iced milk has a great milky, creamy texture that makes it feel substantial; as if you really froze milk into a stick. That said, the flavor is more condensed milk or powdered milk (or the Haw Haw powdered milk candy), and not milk milk. Though it works if you’re a fan of any of the former.



11. CHOCOLATE



The chocolate stick kind of tastes like the chocolate sundae (in the cup), but way better. (Plus extra points because you don’t have to eat it with a spoon.) It’s much creamier, and tastes less artificial. It also has the same nice texture as the other stick variants. Members of the team liken the taste to “a creamier chocolate Twin Popsies,” “a Milo ice cream,” and “the creamy choco in our childhood.”



12. CHOCOLATE SUNDAE



The chocolate sundae is more mousse than it is ice cream, which isn’t at all a bad thing. It’s light, fluffy, and is “half (Dairy Queen) Blizzard, half whipped cream” in texture. “If they named it ‘Chocolate Mousse,’ that’d be [more accurate],” says a member of the team. The chocolate in the ice cream has a chemical-y taste, like the usual artificial chocolate. But the chocolate syrup, which is generously swirled into the sundae, is decent. It’s like cheap chocolate syrup, but the good kind.



13. PINEAPPLE



The pineapple stick is similar in texture as Nestle Twin Popsies. It holds together, and is easy to chew if you’re into that sort of thing. The team describes it as tasting like a slightly watered down version of Jollibee’s pineapple juice.
